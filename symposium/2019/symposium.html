Description:

The ESIP Semantic Technologies Committee is hosting its annual Geosemantics Symposium on January 14th, 2019 in Washington DC as part of the ESIP Winter Meeting. This year's symposium is investigating how to consolidate and align Earth science semantic resources (e.g., ENVO, SWEET, etc.) to take advantage of resources such as schema.org, Google tooling, and other external tooling. The symposium will help launch the Semantic Technologies Committee's effort to develop, manage, and socialize community-based extensions to schema.org vocabularies for dataset landing pages and other science data-related web sites. We want this to be a community-driven process and we need your input! Please join us at the Bethesda North Marriott in Bethesda MD on January 14th. The symposium is free and open to the public. Register here, or email beth@lingualogica.net. Check our webpage from time to time for updates.

We seek broad community input and encourage non-ESIP members and members of other professional societies to attend. The event itself is free; however, attendees will be responsible for providing their own travel and lodging. The exact room within the Marriott is still being worked out and will be provided in future announcements. Also, suggestions for topics to be covered are welcome.
